Hogao: A Colombian Specialty

With this easy recipe, you'll be making a Colombian delicacy in no time! Used as a base for many Colombian dishes or as a topper for foods like arepas or patacones (fried plantain), this delicious staple will bring your meals to the next level.

Prep Time: 10 min

Cook Time: 15 min

Photo Credit: Urban Farm and Kitchen

Ingredients

Required

  • 2 tablespoons of Olive Oil or Vegetable Oil
  • 1 bunch of Green Onions
  • 3 Garlic Cloves
  • 1 teaspoon of Ground Cumin
  • 4 plum tomatoes
  • Kosher salt (to taste)
  • Freshly Cracked Ground Pepper (to taste)

Optional

  • 1/2 teaspoon of Ground Turmeric
  • Cilantro for garnish

Recipe Prep

  1. Green Onions: trim and finely chop
  2. Garlic Cloves: mince and set aside
  3. Plum Tomatoes: dice into size of your choosing (smaller chunks are recommended)

Cooking Instructions

  1. Sautee the base. In a pan set to medium heat, add the oil, onion, garlic, cumin, and turmeric. Fry until soft (approx. 4-6 min)
  2. Add tomatoes, salt, and pepper. Continue to cook on medium-low heat, stirring frequently, for about 6-8 minutes. The sauce should thicken.
  3. Season as needed. Taste and add more salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Add cilantro. Add cilantro (optional) and mix into the sauce for about 1 minute. Turn off the heat when done.
  5. Serve. Serve in a bowl. If storing, hogao can last 3-4 days in the fridge or be frozen for longer storage.
